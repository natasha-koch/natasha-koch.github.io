Context

Mitsubishi Motors Australia needed to replace an outdated wizard-style vehicle configurator with a flexible, non-linear Build & Price tool, one that allowed users to modify selections at any stage of the configuration journey. Unlike traditional transactional ecommerce, the tool was designed around a lead-generation model, guiding users from consumer discovery online through to dealer-assisted conversion in the showroom.

The challenge

From ecommerce to lead generation

I navigated a shift to automotive lead-generation UX, with competitor research across Australian and international Build & Price tools informing the direction. This work ran alongside addressing significant complexity in vehicle configuration, pricing models, and compliance requirements.

Consolidating a complex brief

Despite deep institutional knowledge from the client, early requirements gathering sessions revealed a fragmented view that the cross-functional team had difficulty consolidating effectively.

My approach

Wireframe-driven discovery

Proposed an alternative discovery approach, building out the configuration logic visually using wireframes in conjunction with the client, methodically across sessions. What began as wireframes evolved into a comprehensive component and variation mapping document, covering every configuration state, edge case, pricing display variation (drive away, finance, price on application, and contact dealer), and legal and compliance requirements across all device contexts.

Single source of truth

This became the project's single source of truth, simultaneously serving the business analyst, technical analyst, front-end and back-end developers, and testers in one living document. The BA fed annotations directly into MoSCoW-prioritised user stories and Jira tickets, and the entire team referenced it throughout the build. Testers found it an exceptionally solid foundation for their work.

Strategic handover call

High fidelity UI designs were produced for priority flows to establish the visual direction and quality bar. Alongside this, annotated style tiles were adopted as the primary methodology for documenting component variations, communicating visual intent, interaction states, breakpoint behaviour, and business logic simultaneously to both design and development audiences. As the project scaled and time was of the essence, I made the strategic call to lean into style tiles as the dominant handover approach, accelerating delivery without compromising the integrity of the overall design output.

Client & user validation

I worked in a highly collaborative manner with the client liaison through weekly sessions of two hours or more, using interactive prototypes to present solutions, explore directions in real time, and secure stakeholder alignment at each stage. Wireframes were also presented as prototypes to senior Mitsubishi stakeholders to gain sign-off and confirm project direction. I observed user research sessions and prototype testing conducted by external research partner U1 Group, using findings to validate and refine design decisions. Given full creative freedom to explore different UX directions, iterating rapidly both within and between sessions.

Post-launch UX ownership

Post-launch, I led iterative UX improvements over several months, drawing on Hotjar session analysis to identify friction and behaviour patterns, client-directed priorities, and deferred scope items carried over from the initial build. Improvements ranged from self-initiated deep dives into areas I identified as deserving closer testing, to client-requested focus areas including the header breadcrumb and special offers experience, ensuring the tool continued to improve in the hands of real users.

Delivery under pressure

When front-end development fell behind and I identified a critical delivery risk, I escalated to both the Project Manager and General Manager, not only with the problem but with proposed solutions, including the specific senior developers I trusted to turn it around. The response was immediate: tell me what you need and you'll have it. I orchestrated parallel workstreams, directing another designer to cover outstanding Design Reviews and QA while the focused recovery work was underway.

Working in close designer-developer pairing, we workshopped solutions, balancing design intent against technical constraints to realise the complex animations and interactions and achieve an overall smooth, premium-feel experience. A two-week delay was negotiated with the client, still ensuring the tool launched ahead of the new Triton release.

Outcome

The tool launched successfully, with dealerships reporting it made their job easier, proving its value as an in-showroom sales conversion asset. Key Mitsubishi stakeholders provided strong positive feedback, and the tool contributed to increased lead generation for Mitsubishi. I received direct recognition from the Mitsubishi client liaison for the quality of the design contribution.
